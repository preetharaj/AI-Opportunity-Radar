NSF TechAccess: AI-Ready America (solicitation NSF 26-508) is a joint initiative by NSF, the Department of Labor, USDA NIFA, and the Small Business Administration to fund state-level AI coordination infrastructure across all 50 US states, the District of Columbia, and US territories.

**What the programme funds:**
- State/Territory Coordination Hubs — one per state/territory
- Hubs connect higher education, workforce development, industry, K-12, and local government
- Objective: Expand access to AI tools, training, and knowledge resources statewide
- Emphasis on workforce upskilling, experiential learning, and scalable locally-driven AI adoption

**Funding:**
- Up to $1 million/year per hub for 3 years (possible 4th year continuation)
- Total programme ceiling: $168M–$224M across 56 hubs

**Three competition rounds:**
- **Round 1:** LOI June 16, 2026 → Full proposal July 16, 2026 (10 hubs)
- **Round 2:** LOI December 15, 2026 → Full proposal January 15, 2027 (20 hubs)
- **Round 3:** Full proposal July 1, 2027 (remaining 26 hubs)

**Who should apply:**
- Universities, community colleges, extension services
- Workforce development boards and state agencies
- Economic development organisations
- Must be a US-based entity submitting for a specific state or territory

**Key constraint:** One proposal per institution. Applicants must identify all statewide consortium partners before submitting the LOI.

Contact: ai-ready@nsf.gov
