The Machine Learning Reproducibility Challenge encourages the community to reproduce and scrutinise published ML results — a critical but undervalued contribution to science.

**2026 Key dates:**
- **Intent to submit deadline: June 4, 2026** (23:59 AOE)
- TMLR decision deadline: September 30, 2026
- Accepted paper announcements: October 2026

**How it works:**
1. Pick a recently published ML paper
2. Reproduce its core claims independently
3. Submit a TMLR paper documenting your reproduction (successful or not)
4. Register your intent by June 4

**Why participate:**
- Publication credit in a peer-reviewed venue (TMLR)
- Builds rigorous research skills
- Contributes to ML's scientific integrity

Particularly good for graduate students and early-career researchers looking for a structured publication opportunity.
