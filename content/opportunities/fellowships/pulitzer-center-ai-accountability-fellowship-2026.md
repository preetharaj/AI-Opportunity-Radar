The Pulitzer Center is accepting applications for the fifth cohort of its AI Accountability Fellowships — one of the most prestigious fellowships for journalists covering artificial intelligence.

**What fellows receive:**
- Up to $20,000 for reporting costs (records requests, travel, data analysis, stipends)
- Up to $5,000 for engagement activities
- 10 months of peer community, mentorship, and training
- Access to the AI Accountability Network of 35+ fellows across 29 countries

**Fellowship period:** September 2026 – July 2027

**What they're looking for:**
- In-depth reporting projects on AI systems' societal impacts
- Transparency and accountability in how AI is funded, built, and deployed
- Journalists representing diverse communities affected by AI
- Strong pre-reporting and a specific story concept — generic proposals are quickly spotted

**Useful note from Pulitzer:** They specifically flag that proposals generated or substantially drafted by LLM tools produce "flattened language, lack of specificity, and factual errors" — and are quickly spotted. Write your own proposal.

**Ask Me Anything webinar:** June 11, 2026 — attend for tips from editors and past fellows.

**Deadline: July 12, 2026 at 11:59 PM EDT**
